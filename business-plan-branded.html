01 / Vision & Thesis
The Operating System for Health Agency
Cipher becomes the premium lifestyle brand for people who take their health seriously — the intersection of Whoop's data obsession, Lululemon's cultural gravity, and Equinox's physical experience.
Pioneer the agency movement in health. A world where people own their health — their data, their decisions, their outcomes.

10-year north star: A world where people are active agents running their own experiments and building systems that compound over decades. Not passive patients waiting for something to break.

Thesis

Start with media and coaching (digital, low-capital), use revenue and audience to fund expansion into products (supplements, apparel), then franchise the physical experience (health centers). Every phase earns its way to the next. No phase launches on hope — it launches on proof.

Positioning

Premium but accessible. Data-driven but human. "Decode what works for you" as the through-line across every product.

Not This
Wellness Pastels
Goop, Calm — too soft, too passive, too vague
Not This
Bro Performance
Most fitness brands — too loud, too dogmatic, too short-term
Not This
Clinical Tech
Parsley, Forward — too institutional, too expensive, too dependent
Cipher is the sharp strategist with warmth — contrarian, evidence-based, personal, and built for people who think in systems.

Brand North Star

Agency. Every product, piece of content, and experience should make people more capable of owning their health. The measure of Cipher's success isn't how much clients depend on us — it's how much they don't need to.

Target Customer

HENRYs — High Earners Not Rich Yet. 30-45, data-driven professionals. They already own an Oura ring or Whoop. They've read Huberman and listened to Attia. They have the data and the desire — but not the synthesis. They're drowning in information with no system for turning it into action.

05 / The Cipher Method
The Unifying IP
The diagnostic framework, experiment methodology, and coaching protocol that runs through every single vertical. The certifiable, franchisable, defensible intellectual property at the center of the empire.

The 7-Dimension Diagnostic

Every client (and eventually every app user) starts with a comprehensive diagnostic:

Sleep
Architecture, timing, environment, quality vs. quantity
Nutrition
Dietary patterns, metabolic response, relationship with food
Movement
Exercise type/frequency, recovery, functional capacity
Recovery & Stress
HRV patterns, stress management, recovery practices
Data & Tracking
Current tools, data literacy, interpretation ability
Mindset & Beliefs
Health beliefs, experimentation readiness, agency level
Lifestyle & Constraints
Work demands, relationships, environment, time budgets

The Experiment Cycle

Diagnose → Hypothesize → Experiment → Measure → Synthesize → Repeat ↻

Each 4-week experiment cycle:

  1. Week 1: Review prior cycle. Select focus. Define hypothesis, metrics, protocol.
  2. Week 2-3: Execute. Daily tracking. Midpoint check-in. Adjust if needed.
  3. Week 4: Measure results vs. hypothesis. Document learnings. Plan next cycle.

The power is in the compounding: each experiment builds on prior ones. After 6-12 months, a client has a personalized, evidence-backed health system that no generic protocol could provide.

06 / Competitive Moat
The Moat Stack
No single moat wins. The strategy is stacking moats so that replicating Cipher requires replicating all of them simultaneously.
Moat 1
Brand
"Sharp strategist with warmth" occupies a unique position. The Signal visual language, agency philosophy, and authentic voice create differentiation that can't be copied.
Moat 2
Methodology
Documented, certifiable, and evolving. Certification creates a distributed network of practitioners who reinforce the same methodology.
Moat 3
Data Flywheel
More users = more patterns = better AI = better recommendations. This compounding advantage can't be replicated without years of data.
Moat 4
Community
Network effects: the community gets more valuable as it grows. A member in Austin learns from experiments in London. Hard to build, harder to leave.
Moat 5
Content
Years of content, audience relationships, and trust built through consistent publishing. You can't fast-forward audience trust.
Moat 6
Physical Presence
Health centers create real estate barriers, local community ties, and experiences that digital-only competitors can't match.

07 / Key Risks & Mitigations
Eyes Open
Every ambitious plan has risks. These are the ones that could kill the business — and how we prevent each one.
Risk 01 Bottleneck on Jordan
Everything depends on one person — content, coaching, methodology, brand, decisions.
Mitigation
Certification trains others to deliver. AI coaching scales methodology. Content library grows as evergreen assets. Strong operators hired early.
Risk 02 Capital Intensity of Physical
Health centers require $500K-$2M buildout per location. Could overextend.
Mitigation
Franchise model (operators fund buildout). Company-owned flagship proves economics first. No expansion until digital revenue covers overhead.
Risk 03 Supplement Market Crowding
Market is saturated. AG1, Momentous, Thorne, hundreds of DTC brands.
Mitigation
Tied to methodology, not sold in isolation. App recommends based on diagnostics. Transparent formulations. Media provides built-in distribution. Only 3 products initially.
Risk 04 Apparel Inventory Risk
Tight margins. Overproduction kills companies.
Mitigation
Limited drops only. Pre-order model for early drops. No retail until DTC proven. Minimal SKUs (2-3 per drop).
Risk 05 Over-Extension Across Verticals
Five businesses at once dilutes focus and quality.
Mitigation
Sequential, not parallel. Phase gates with exit criteria. Media and coaching only for 12+ months. Kill underperforming verticals.
Risk 06 The Peloton Lesson
Growth-phase overinvestment in fixed costs creates existential risk when growth slows.
Mitigation
Capital-efficient at every phase. Franchise for physical, contract manufacturing for supps. Variable cost structure. Model 50% revenue decline scenarios. Bootstrap is the default.
Risk 07 Regulatory and Liability
Health coaching gray zone. Supplement claims FDA-regulated. Bad outcomes create liability.
Mitigation
Clear scope: coaching, not medical advice. Explicit agreements and terms. FDA-compliant supplement claims. Professional and product liability insurance. Legal review of all client-facing materials.
