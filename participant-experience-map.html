Foundation

Product Architecture

Cipher is a health intelligence platform with three layers:

Coach Layer Tier 2 + 3 AI Coach: proactive accountability, personalized guidance, real-time support. AI+Human: emergence reviews, deep context.
Experiment Engine All Tiers Skill tree progression across health domains. 4-week cycles. Montessori-style: assessment → choice → experiment → emerge.
Data Platform All Tiers Health data hub. Wearables, nutrition, labs, subjective. On par or better than Apple Health. Everything in one place.
Key Principle The platform is useful at every layer. Data aggregation alone has value. The experiment engine adds structure. The coach layer adds acceleration. Each tier stands on its own — coaching doesn’t prop up a weak platform.
Philosophy

Behavioral Philosophy

Cipher’s approach to change draws from multiple frameworks, blended into a model that matches the N-of-1 philosophy.

Core Principles

1. Autonomy + Competence (Self-Determination Theory)

People change when they feel ownership over the process and see themselves getting better at it. Cipher’s entire design reinforces this: participants choose their experiments, interpret their own data over time, and build mastery. The product’s job is to make them NOT need the product — “you ARE the emergent intelligence.”

2. Environment Design (Clear / Thaler)

Make the right thing easy. The product itself IS the environment. The dashboard is nudge architecture, not just data display. The skill tree surfaces the next logical experiment. The journal is frictionless enough to actually use. Every UX decision asks: “Does this make the healthy behavior easier or harder?”

3. Emergent Learning Over Delivered Knowledge

Unlike Precision Nutrition (pre-written curriculum delivered to everyone), Cipher generates understanding FROM the participant’s own experience. Knowledge is discovered through experiments, not delivered through lessons. The data tells the story — the product helps you read it.

4. Progressive Mastery Through Structured Choice

Like a Montessori classroom or Alpha School: the environment is structured, the progression is designed, but the learner chooses their path through it. Foundation skills unlock more advanced experiments. Cross-domain connections emerge as breadth increases. Every 4 weeks is a decision point informed by data and experience.

Engine

The Skill Tree System

The experiment engine is organized as interconnected skill trees across health domains. Each domain has a progression from foundation habits to advanced experiments, with cross-domain nodes that unlock when you have sufficient experience in multiple areas.

Health Domains

Domain Foundation Focus Example Progression
Sleep Consistent schedule, tracking → Environment optimization → Light/temp protocols → Cross-domain (caffeine, exercise timing)
Nutrition Awareness, protein targets → Meal timing → Micronutrient focus → Metabolic response (CGM) → Cross-domain (sleep, performance)
Movement Consistent baseline, step count → Training structure → Recovery integration → Cross-domain (sleep quality, HRV response)
Stress & Recovery Awareness, one practice → Breathwork/meditation progression → HRV-guided recovery → Cross-domain (sleep, performance, relationships)
Social-Emotional Self-awareness, journaling → Communication skills → Boundary setting → Cross-domain (stress, recovery, environment)
Relationships Connection audit, quality time → Support network building → Conflict resolution → Cross-domain (stress, emotional health)
Environment Sleep environment, workspace → Light exposure → Air quality → Digital environment → Cross-domain (sleep, focus, stress)

Skill Tree Structure

Each domain follows the same pattern:

DOMAIN SKILL TREE ██ Foundations (required, 1-2 experiments) ├─ █ Baseline tracking (establish data) ├─ █ Core habit (the one thing that matters most) └─ █ Consistency check (can you sustain it?) │ ██ Environment & Systems (pick path, 2-3 experiments) ├─ □ Option A ──┐ ├─ □ Option B ──┴─> □ Advanced Integration └─ □ Option C ──┘ │ ██ Cross-Domain (unlocks with experience in other domains) ├─ □ Domain × Sleep [req: Sleep L1] ├─ □ Domain × Nutrition [req: Nutrition L1] └─ □ Domain × Stress [req: Recovery L1]

How Participants Navigate

  1. Assessment maps their current state across all domains (diagnostic intake form + connected data)
  2. System recommends a starting domain and experiment based on assessment — highest leverage or easiest win
  3. Participant chooses — agency always wins over optimization. They pick the domain and experiment that resonates
  4. 4-week experiment cycle — hypothesis → protocol → daily tracking → emergence review
  5. Decision point — at the end of each cycle, the skill tree updates. New nodes unlock. Participant chooses next direction informed by data AND experience
  6. Cross-domain connections emerge naturally as they accumulate experiments across domains — the AI surfaces correlations, but they discover the meaning

Progression Visibility

The skill tree is a visible map — participants can see:

  • Where they’ve been (completed experiments)
  • Where they are (active experiment)
  • Where they could go (unlocked nodes)
  • What’s locked (requires prerequisites)
  • Cross-domain connections (experiments that span multiple domains)
Intrinsic Motivation “I can see the whole map. I’m choosing my path through it. And every experiment I complete unlocks new territory.”
Engagement

Adaptive Engagement Model

The product gets quieter as participants get more capable. Inspired by the best of Rise (personalized daily energy insights), Finch (multi-layered engagement loops), and Calm (progressive skill building) — while avoiding their mistakes.

Engagement Phases

Phase When Daily Touchpoints Product Behavior
Onboarding Week 1-2 3-4 (morning signal, midday prompt, evening reflection, data sync notification) Active guidance. “Here’s what to focus on today.” Hand-holding is appropriate — they’re learning the system.
Building Week 3-8 2-3 (morning signal, evening reflection, experiment-related prompts) Structured support. Daily rhythm established. Product reinforces the experiment cycle.
Practicing Month 3-6 1-2 (morning signal, contextual nudges only when data warrants) Selective engagement. Product speaks up for anomalies, milestones, and decision points. Quieter otherwise.
Independent Month 6+ Weekly digest + on-demand The product is a tool they use, not a teacher that talks at them. Weekly intelligence summary. They drive their own cadence.

Design Principles (Learned from Product Research)

From Rise

Personalized daily insights tied to YOUR data. Energy wave visualization. Contextual suggestions (bedtime recommendations, nap windows). The gold standard for “useful without being annoying.”

From Finch

Multi-layered engagement loops (hourly, daily, weekly, monthly) create sustained engagement. But — the gamification must serve the BEHAVIOR, not become the behavior. Finch’s mistake was making the pet more compelling than the actual habits. Cipher’s engagement loops must always point back to the experiment and the data.

From Calm

Progressive skill building works. Streaks create consistency. But — monetization cannot corrupt the experience. When every new feature is a paywall, trust erodes. Cipher’s freemium model must feel generous at the free tier.

From HiCoffee

Simple, specific, actionable. Show ONE thing mapped to YOUR schedule. The caffeine half-life visualization is a masterclass in making data personally useful. Cipher should aspire to this level of clarity for every insight.

From Levels

Real-time feedback loops drive the fastest behavioral change. But multi-app friction kills habit formation. Cipher must be the single surface — no “open three apps to understand one thing.”

From MacroFactor

Simplicity wins. Friction is the enemy of tracking adherence. The adaptive algorithm (adjusting targets based on actual data) is the model for how Cipher’s AI should work — learn from the participant’s real results, not just initial assumptions.

Anti-Patterns to Avoid

Traps Finch trap: gamification that becomes the goal instead of the behavior
Calm trap: monetization that degrades the core experience
Levels trap: requiring multiple apps for one workflow
PN trap: one-size curriculum with no personalization or data integration

Intelligence

Product Lessons from the Field

Synthesized from Jordan’s direct experience with these products. These lessons inform every product decision.

Precision Nutrition Pro Coach

What worked

Habit sequencing — master mindfulness → master environment → master each nutrition area (protein, vegetables, fruits, smart carbs, healthy fats) → tactical practices. The progressive layering was sound. Daily check-ins created rhythm. Habit chaining built real foundation.

What Cipher takes

The sequencing principle. Foundation habits before complex ones. A clear progression path. Daily check-in rhythm.

What Cipher fixes
  • No data integration — PN was entirely self-report and pre-written curriculum
  • No personalization — everyone got the same sequence regardless of starting point
  • Thin coach experience — coaches flew blind between check-ins
  • No emergent learning — knowledge was delivered, not discovered
  • Graduation was hollow — just ran out of lessons, not a mastery moment
  • Single domain — nutrition and exercise only, not whole health
Cipher’s model

Same progressive sequencing principle, but with a Montessori-style decision tree across ALL health domains, informed by real data, where the curriculum emerges from the participant’s own experiments.

Oura

What worked

Readiness score as a single synthesized signal. Sleep staging visualization makes complex data intuitive. Long-term trend views reveal patterns invisible day-to-day. Best-in-class passive data collection.

What Cipher takes

Synthesized scores. Intuitive data visualization. Trend-over-time as the primary analytical lens.

What Cipher fixes

No coach or accountability layer beyond social circles. No experiment framework — just data display. No cross-domain synthesis.

Levels (CGM)

What worked

Real-time feedback loops — seeing glucose spike after a meal creates the fastest behavioral learning loop in health tech. Food + glucose overlay is an experiment built into the UX.

What Cipher takes

Immediate cause-and-effect feedback when data supports it. The “experiment you don’t know you’re running” UX pattern.

What Cipher fixes

Overly complicated UI. Multi-app friction (CGM app + Levels app + food logger). No micronutrient data or cross-tracker synthesis. Hardware reliability issues broke the habit loop.

MacroFactor

What worked

Simplicity wins. Fast food logging is the #1 predictor of adherence. Adaptive algorithm that adjusts targets based on actual weight trends — the system learns from YOUR data, not just initial assumptions.

What Cipher takes

Friction is the enemy. Adaptive algorithms that learn from real results. The principle that a system should get smarter the more you use it.

What Cipher fixes

Quick-track should be camera/voice-first, not buried behind clicks. The future is ambient capture (AR glasses + voice confirmation).

Rise Sleep

What worked

Best-in-class personalized daily insights tied to YOUR data. Energy level waves throughout the day with suggestions on bedtimes. Customized per-day insights. Shows how your current sleep is affecting health and performance RIGHT NOW.

What Cipher takes

The energy wave visualization model. Personalized, time-specific insights. Making data personally actionable (“your energy will dip at 2pm — nap window”).

HiCoffee

What worked

Simple, specific, actionable. Shows ONE thing (caffeine half-life) mapped to YOUR schedule. A masterclass in making data personally useful without overwhelming.

What Cipher takes

Every insight should aspire to this level of clarity and personal specificity.

Finch

What worked

Best layered engagement loops — hourly, daily, weekly, monthly engagement tiers with escalating time investment. Creates sustained engagement across multiple timeframes.

What Cipher takes

Multi-layered engagement loops. Different cadences for different purposes.

What Cipher fixes

The gamification became the goal. Users optimized their Finch pet instead of their actual behavior. The virtual pet was more compelling than the habits it was supposed to support. Cipher’s engagement loops must always point back to the experiment and the real data. The skill tree IS the game — but the rewards are real insights about your body, not virtual achievements.

Calm

What worked

Progressive skill building. Streaks create consistency. The daily Calm meditation created ritual.

What Cipher takes

Progressive difficulty. Streak mechanics for consistency (used sparingly). Ritual-quality daily touchpoints.

What Cipher fixes

Monetization corrupted the experience. Every new feature became a paywall. Users wanted a simple timer with light guidance and sounds — Calm tried to sell guided meditations instead. Cipher’s free tier must feel genuinely generous.

Bevel Health

What worked

Closest to Cipher’s data aggregation vision. Synthesizes across wearables and biomarkers into a unified health view — the synthesis layer most products lack.

What Cipher takes

The data synthesis model. Multi-source aggregation as the foundation.

What Cipher adds

AI-enabled coaching layer on top. Bevel is the data — Cipher is the data + the intelligence + the experiment engine + the coach.
