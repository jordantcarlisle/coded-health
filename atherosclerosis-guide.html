Pathophysiology

What Is Atherosclerosis

Atherosclerosis is a chronic inflammatory disease of the arterial wall — the accumulation of lipids, immune cells, and fibrous material forming plaques within the intima of medium and large arteries. It is the leading cause of death globally, underlying the majority of heart attacks, strokes, and peripheral vascular disease.

The process begins decades before symptoms appear. By age 36, most adults have some degree of subclinical disease. The critical question is not if plaque is present but how much, what type, and what can be done about it.

How It Develops

Stage 1: Endothelial Dysfunction

Triggered by oxidized LDL, hypertension, smoking, or hyperglycemia. The endothelium becomes permeable to atherogenic lipoproteins (ApoB-containing particles).

Stage 2: Fatty Streak Formation

ApoB-containing lipoproteins (LDL, VLDL remnants, Lp(a)) penetrate the arterial wall, oxidize, and trigger monocyte recruitment. Monocytes become macrophages that engulf oxidized lipids — forming foam cells.

Stage 3: Fibroatheroma

Smooth muscle cells migrate into the intima, producing collagen and forming a fibrous cap over the lipid-rich necrotic core. This is the classic "plaque."

Stage 4: Advanced / Vulnerable Plaque

Continued lipid accumulation, necrotic core expansion, calcification. Vulnerable plaques have thin fibrous caps, large lipid cores, and active inflammation — these are the ones that rupture and cause heart attacks.

Evidence

Atherosclerosis Can Be Reversed

Lipid-rich (soft) plaques have the highest therapeutic potential for regression. Clinical trials using intravascular ultrasound and optical coherence tomography have demonstrated actual plaque volume reduction with aggressive intervention.

Analysis

Key Interpretation

1. ApoB-Driven Risk

ApoB at 107 and LDL-P at 1973 are concordantly elevated. Per Thomas Dayspring's concordance framework, when both are elevated, the risk signal is clear and unambiguous. ApoB is the single best predictor — it counts every atherogenic particle in your blood.

2. Lp(a) Amplifies Lifetime Risk

At 103 nmol/L, Lp(a) is an independent, genetically-determined risk multiplier. It contributes atherogenic particles not captured by standard LDL-C. With elevated Lp(a), the ApoB target becomes even more aggressive — ideally <50 mg/dL to compensate for the unmodifiable Lp(a) burden.

3. CAC >0 at 36 Confirms Plaque

A CAC of 2 is low but not zero. Any calcium at this age means atherosclerotic plaque has already developed and begun to calcify. This reclassifies risk upward regardless of what 10-year risk calculators say. It also means the intervention window is now — before further calcification.

4. Low Inflammation Is a Positive

hsCRP at 0.7 is excellent. The atherogenic process here appears lipid-driven rather than inflammation-driven — which means aggressive lipid reduction should be highly effective.

5. Metabolic Health Is Reasonable

Triglycerides at 103 and Pattern A suggest insulin resistance is not severe. HOMA-IR and fasting insulin should be measured to confirm, but the metabolic picture is encouraging.

Lifestyle

Nutrition

The Portfolio Diet achieves LDL reductions of 28-30% in controlled trials — comparable to low-dose statins. Combined with Mediterranean principles, it's the most powerful dietary intervention available.

The Portfolio Diet — Five Pillars

1. Nuts & Seeds — 1 oz almonds/day (23 almonds)

LDL reduction: 5-7%. Evidence: Strong. Tree nuts reduce total and LDL cholesterol through phytosterol content and displacement of saturated fat.

2. Plant Proteins — 50g soy protein/day

LDL reduction: 3-5%. Evidence: Strong. Tofu, tempeh, edamame, soy milk. FDA-approved health claim for soy and heart disease reduction.

3. Viscous Fiber — 18g soluble fiber/day

LDL reduction: 5-7% (oat beta-glucan) + ~13 mg/dL (psyllium). Evidence: Strong. Oatmeal, barley, psyllium husk, legumes. Binds bile acids, forcing liver to pull LDL from blood.

4. Plant Sterols — 2g/day

LDL reduction: 6-12%. Evidence: Strong. Block intestinal cholesterol absorption. Available as supplements or in fortified foods.

5. Monounsaturated Fats — EVOO as primary fat

2+ tablespoons/day of extra virgin olive oil. Anti-inflammatory, improves endothelial function. Evidence: Strong (PREDIMED trial).

The Stacking Principle These foods produce additive LDL reductions. Combined Portfolio Diet + Mediterranean principles can achieve 20-35% LDL reduction — the whole is greater than the sum of the parts.

Lifestyle

Exercise Protocols

Moving from the bottom 25th percentile to above the 75th percentile in VO2 max is associated with a 5x reduction in all-cause mortality risk. Exercise is the most powerful longevity intervention available.

Zone 2 Cardio — The Foundation

3-4 sessions/week · 45-60 minutes each · 3-4 hours/week minimum

The highest metabolic output you can sustain while holding a conversation. Typically 60-70% max HR (~111-129 bpm at age 36). Drives mitochondrial biogenesis, fat oxidation, insulin sensitivity, and metabolic flexibility.

Modalities: Cycling, brisk walking/hiking, rowing, swimming, elliptical. NWA's trail systems are ideal Zone 2 terrain.

VO2 Max Training — The Strongest Mortality Predictor

1-2 sessions/week · 4×4 minute intervals at 90-95% max HR

3-4 minute active recovery between intervals. A randomized trial showed 6 months of supervised HIIT achieved coronary atheroma regression measured by IVUS. VO2 max is one of the strongest predictors of all-cause mortality — prioritize this.

Resistance Training — The Longevity Reserve

2-4 sessions/week · 30-60 minutes · All major muscle groups

AHA 2023 endorses resistance training for cardiovascular health. 23% reduction in CHD risk with at least 30 min/week. Focus on progressive overload. Significant CVD benefit below 120 min/week; no additional cardiovascular benefit above that.

Sleep is not passive recovery — it is an active cardiovascular intervention. Sleep fragmentation increases leukocyte production in bone marrow, directly accelerating atherosclerotic inflammation. Both short (<7 hrs) and long (>9 hrs) sleep increase risk of heart failure, MI, and hypertension.

Lifestyle

Stress Management

Chronic stress releases glucocorticoids that increase bone marrow white blood cell production, flooding arterial walls with inflammatory cells that destabilize plaques. Reduced resting HRV (SDNN <70ms) is associated with 1.5-2.3x higher risk of major adverse cardiovascular events.

Intervention Evidence Notes
Meditation / mindfulness Moderate One RCT: transcendental meditation reduced CIMT progression over 7 months. Improves cortisol, HRV, inflammatory markers.
Breathwork Moderate Box breathing, 4-7-8 technique, physiological sigh. Activates parasympathetic nervous system.
Nature exposure Moderate Documented cortisol reductions. NWA's trail systems are a significant advantage.
Social connection Strong Social isolation is a CVD risk factor comparable to smoking.

Pharmacological Options

When lifestyle alone is insufficient to reach regression-level targets. Given ApoB at 107 with a target of <60 (a 44% reduction) and elevated Lp(a), pharmacological intervention deserves serious discussion — not as failure of lifestyle, but as a complementary tool.

High-intensity statin (atorvastatin 40-80mg, rosuvastatin 20-40mg) 50-60% ASTEROID: plaque regression by IVUS Foundation therapy. At age 36 with CAC >0 and elevated Lp(a), lifetime benefit is substantial. Add CoQ10 + K2 if initiated.
Ezetimibe +18-25% (on statin) IMPROVE-IT: incremental CV benefit confirmed Blocks intestinal cholesterol absorption. Well-tolerated.
PCSK9 inhibitor (evolocumab, alirocumab) +50-60% (on statin) GLAGOV: plaque regression on IVUS Also lowers Lp(a) 20-30% — relevant given your Lp(a) of 103. Injectable q2-4 weeks.
Icosapent ethyl (Vascepa) 4g/day TG focus REDUCE-IT: 25% CVD risk reduction Anti-inflammatory, plaque stabilization. Benefits beyond TG lowering.
Low-dose colchicine (0.5mg/day) Anti-inflammatory LoDoCo2, COLCOT First FDA-approved anti-inflammatory for ASCVD. Targets NLRP3 inflammasome.
Bempedoic acid ~18% CLEAR Outcomes Non-statin option. No myalgias (inactive in muscle). For statin-intolerant patients.
The Honest Math Best-case dietary LDL reduction: 25-35% (Portfolio Diet + full stacking). This brings ApoB from ~107 to ~70-80 — still above the <60 target, and well above the <50 ideal given elevated Lp(a). Lifestyle is essential and foundational, but the biomarker profile strongly suggests lifestyle + pharmacological intervention together will produce the best outcome. Early aggressive ApoB lowering at 36, before further calcification, is the highest-leverage window.
Horizon

Emerging Research

GLP-1 Receptor Agonists (semaglutide, tirzepatide)

Originally diabetes/obesity drugs, now showing direct anti-atherosclerotic effects. SELECT trial: semaglutide reduced MACE by 20% in obese non-diabetic patients. Upregulates antioxidant enzymes. Strong & growing

Novel Lp(a)-Lowering Therapies

Especially relevant given Lp(a) of 103 nmol/L. Zerlasiran: >80% reduction (RNA interference). Obicetrapib: 54% reduction (CETP inhibitor). VERVE-102: CRISPR gene editing for permanent PCSK9 inactivation. Phase 2-3, potentially available within 2-3 years. Emerging

New Imaging: Perivascular Fat Attenuation Index

CT-derived biomarker that identifies inflamed coronary arteries and vulnerable plaques by phenotyping perivascular fat. Non-invasive detection of plaque vulnerability before events occur. Emerging
