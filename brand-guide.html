01 / Overview
The Brand
Cipher is a health optimization coaching business for data-driven professionals who collect health data but can't connect the dots. We provide the human layer — personalized experiments, data synthesis, and accountability.

Vision

Pioneer the agency movement in health. A world where people own their health — their data, their decisions, their outcomes. Active agents running their own experiments, not passive patients waiting for something to break.

Mission

Help high-performing individuals move from overwhelmed to empowered through personalized experiments, data synthesis, and human partnership — so they can take control of their health for life.

Core Values

Honesty Over Comfort
Tell hard truths. No hype. Even if it means saying "this won't work for you."
Long-Term Over Quick Wins
Refuse shortcuts. Build sustainable change. Identity-based transformation, not 30-day challenges.
Empowerment Over Dependence
Success means they need you less. Teach people to run their own experiments.

05 / Voice & Tone
How Cipher Sounds
A sharp friend who happens to be a health strategist — direct enough to challenge you, warm enough that you trust them, curious enough to never stop experimenting.

Three Voice Rules

Rule 1
Lead with Signal, Not Noise

Every piece of content should cut something away. If you're not making something clearer, simpler, or more actionable — don't publish it.

Rule 2
Show the Experiment, Not the Answer

Share your process, not your conclusions. "I tested X and here's what happened" beats "You should do X" every time.

Rule 3
You're the Guide, Not the Guru

The client is the hero. Position yourself beside them, slightly ahead — never above. Empowerment over dependence.

Writing Principles

Do

  • Share specific numbers and data
  • Acknowledge what you don't know
  • Ask a question at the end — content is a conversation
  • Frame everything as an experiment, not a prescription
  • Credit sources ("Attia talks about this...")
  • Make the reader the hero — "you" more than "I"
  • Cut the first paragraph — your real opening is usually your second

Don't

  • Say "biohack" or "hack your health" — implies shortcuts
  • Say "you should" — prescriptive guru energy
  • Say "optimize your life" — robotic, overused
  • Say "crushing it" or "beast mode" — performative
  • Say "quick results" — contradicts the long game
  • Say "my protocol" — implies one-size-fits-all
  • Pretend you have all the answers

Tone Spectrum

The voice stays consistent. The tone shifts by context.

Context Tone Energy
Replying to top voices (Attia, Huberman) Sharp, substantive, peer-level "Adding to the conversation, not applauding from below"
Replying to peers and builders Collaborative, curious, generous "I've been thinking about this too — here's what I found"
Replying to potential clients Warm, empowering, direct "You're closer than you think. Here's the one thing I'd try"
Original posts (health/data) Contrarian, evidence-based, personal "Everyone says X. I tested it. Here's what actually happened"
Original posts (worldview) Bold, philosophical, accessible "The future of health isn't what most people think"
Website copy Confident, clean, no fluff "Your health has a pattern. Let's find it."
DMs / outreach Human, low-pressure, respectful "Not selling — genuinely curious if this resonates"

Voice in Action

X Post — Contrarian Insight

Everyone tracks sleep. Almost nobody tracks the 3 things that actually determine sleep quality:

Light exposure timing
Core body temperature curve
Last meal timing relative to melatonin onset

Your Oura score is the output. These are the inputs.

X Post — N=1 Experiment

I wore a CGM for 60 days while changing nothing else about my diet.

The most surprising finding: my glucose spiked more from stress than from any food I ate.

One stressful meeting = worse than a bowl of pasta.

Your metabolic health isn't just a nutrition problem.

Reply to Tier 1 — Add Depth

Interesting framing. I'd push back slightly on the sleep duration emphasis.

I tracked sleep architecture (not just duration) for 90 days. Nights with 6.5 hrs but 2+ hrs of deep sleep consistently outperformed 8 hr nights with fragmented deep sleep — on HRV, subjective energy, and next-day cognitive tests.

Duration is the wrong signal. Architecture is the right one.

Reply to Potential Client — Empower

You're actually further along than you think.

The fact that you're tracking and asking "what does this mean?" puts you ahead of 95% of people. The next step isn't more data — it's designing one small experiment with what you already have.

Pick one variable. Change it for 2 weeks. Watch what moves. That's the whole game.

07 / Origin Story
The Cipher Story
For the About page, pitch decks, podcast intros, and anywhere the brand needs a human behind it.
"I realized I could write my own story. That the narratives I'd been handed were wrong."

Senior year of high school. I'm sitting in class but I can't hear the person speaking right in front of me. Not because the room is loud — because I'm so exhausted my brain has stopped processing sound.

Not sleeping. Constantly overwhelmed. Heart always pounding. Brain fog so thick I couldn't think three steps ahead. We struggled financially, but I had access to schools and networks where people lived very differently. I could see what life looked like on the other side — and that was enough to know the gap was crossable.

But knowing it was crossable didn't matter if my body wouldn't cooperate. I couldn't focus. I couldn't sleep. I couldn't think.

So I wrote it down. All of it. A 50-year vision for my life in a notebook. Then I listed every habit that the person who reached that vision would have. And every habit I needed to let go of. Every day, I tracked whether I did them or not.

It wasn't sophisticated. It was just a notebook and intent. But it built something I didn't have before: awareness.

By senior year of college, something deeper shifted. I'd been reading — not blog posts, but real research. People who had figured out how to build themselves into something different than where they started. And I realized: the story I'd been handed — "you are what you come from" — was wrong.

I had agency. More agency than I'd ever believed.

I started my first company that same year. Tried to build a platform for health coaches because I kept losing touch with mine when I moved around for school. It didn't work. But through that failure, I found the thing that's driven everything since:

Build and use technology to empower people to be true stewards of their health — not passive participants.

Not patients. Not followers. Stewards. People who run experiments, interpret their own data, and make decisions without waiting for permission.

That's what Cipher is.

I spent years learning how to decode my own biology. How to find the signal in the noise. How to turn wearables and data and research into a system that actually works for me — not a generic protocol someone else wrote.

Now I help other people do the same.

Not by giving you my answers. By teaching you how to find your own. Through experiments. Through data you already have. Through the kind of partnership that pushes you to see patterns you've been missing.

Your health has a code. It's not hidden in some guru's protocol or the latest device. It's in you. In your patterns. In the way your body responds when you actually pay attention.

Let's decode it.

09 / Visual Direction
Design Principles
The visual language for all Cipher touchpoints — website, social media, presentations, client materials.

The Direction

The visual language is built around finding the signal in noise — waveforms, frequency patterns, data channels. The concept is executed boldly, not as background decoration. Alive and kinetic energy.

Key Visual Elements

  • Animated waveforms — large-scale, sweep across hero sections
  • Signal readout panels — instrument-panel-style section breaks
  • Noise-to-signal transformation — before/after (static vs. clean)
  • Scanning dots — glowing colored dots along waveform paths
  • Channel indicators — color-coded health domains
  • Gradient color bar — four-color gradient for accents

What It's Not

Not clinical wellness
No white coats, stethoscopes, or sterile stock photography
Not soft pastels
No sage green, dusty rose, or meditation-app energy
Not bro performance
No neon, dark gym photography, or "beast mode" energy
Not generic SaaS
No abstract blobs, gradient backgrounds, or illustration libraries
